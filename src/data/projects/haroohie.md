The Haroohie Translation Club is a project that changed my life. Starting with a random GBATemp forum thread in 2021 and
most recently culminating with the 2026 release of the _Suzumiya Haruhi no Chokuretsu_ English patch, the club's accomplishments
speak for themselves. On a personal level, the club has introduced me to some of my closest friends (including people from all over
the world I would have never met otherwise), inspired me to improve my Japanese (which led to me living in Tokyo), and spawned from
it essentially every other project listed on this site. The genesis for it all came from the passion that so many showed in responding
to a random Discord ping from a Haruhicord admin inviting them to join my little project.

Most relevantly, the Haruhi website is [where I host my blog posts](https://haroohie.club/author/jonko), which in turn led to me
panelling at conventions and starting [my YouTube channel](/#/my-projects/youtube). The [club's site](https://haroohie.club/) is
the best source for information about it, but here's a brief list of our past, current, and upcoming projects. (The projects are listed
in the order we plan to translate them.)

<iconheader title="The Series of Haruhi Suzumiya (Chokuretsu)" icon="cib:nintendo-3ds"></iconheader>

_Chokuretsu_ (Nintendo DS, 2009) is the project that started it all&mdash;the entire club was founded to translate this one game. It took us four years,
but we did in fact finally do it: the full game can now finally be played in English from start to finish. There are still other projects
in flight to translate the game into languages such as Italian and Brazilian Portuguese.

[NitroPacker](/#/my-projects/nitro-packer) predated this project, but was open sourced and improved as a result of it. 
Indeed, after years of hacking the game, [William278](https://william278.net) ended up putting the knowledge we'd gained to use in the creation of the first
editor of [the Loopy Suite](/#/my-projects/loopy-suite): Serial Loops.

I've written several blog posts about hacking _Chokuretsu_, as well as including details on it in the panels I've done, making it my most-documented hacking
project to date. Look forward to more in the future!

<iconheader title="The Parallel of Haruhi Suzumiya (Heiretsu)" icon="cib:wii"></iconheader>

_Heiretsu_ (Wii, 2009) is currently in-flight and has actually been worked on for several years in parallel (heh) with Chokuretsu. Hacking progress was initially
very speedy despite quite a few challenging bumps in the road that will be documented in future blog posts or YouTube videos. Once Millie finished with the base
translation for Chokuretsu, she quickly powered through much of Heiretsu's translation as well. However, much remains to be done, particularly on the front of
reverse-engineering the 3D cutscene format so we can replace the title drop and reverse-engineering more of the data files.

We don't have a planned release date for Heiretsu quite yet, but I'd say you should keep your eyes on Q4 2026/Q1 2027 for some interesting updates!

<iconheader title="The Excitement of Haruhi Suzumiya (Gekidou)" icon="cib:wii"></iconheader>

_Gekidou_ (Wii, 2009) is the odd one out for our translation efforts. A dancing game, it doesn't fit neatly into the mould of visual novels that we mostly have been
translating. However, we have managed to fully dump the script for this game and have a developer working on tooling for it. It's possible that we will release it
with or shortly after _Heiretsu_, but no promises!

<iconheader title="The Reminiscence of Haruhi Suzumiya (Tsuisou)" icon="cib:playstation3" icon2="simple-icons:playstationportable"></iconheader>

_Tsuisou_ (PS3/PSP, 2011) is the project we will be tackling next after _Heiretsu_ is finished. It presents an interesting challenge in that it is a game for both
PSP and PS3, meaning ASM hacks in particular will be challenging since they will have to be ported to both platforms. However, we're aided by [LetsPlayHaruhi](https://www.youtube.com/LetsPlayHaruhi),
who has kindly offered to lend us her scripts that she's been working on for over a decade. This will be used as the base translation to accelerate the game's release.

<iconheader title="The Perplexity of Haruhi Suzumiya (Tomadoi)" icon="simple-icons:playstation2"></iconheader>

_Tomadoi_ (PS2, 2008) is a game that I booted up a few years ago and went "oh no, an immediate cutscene with no subtitles!" However, since then, I've mastered the art
of adding subtitles to things, so hopefully it won't be an issue! Look forward to this one after _Tsuisou_ is complete.

<iconheader title="Suzumiya Haruhi-chan no Mahjong" icon="simple-icons:playstationportable"></iconheader>

_Mahjong_ (PSP, 2011) is currently our lowest priority of the untranslated games, but that didn't stop KahnerC from having a crack at hacking it anyway. We'll likely release this
one after _Tomadoi_.

<iconheader title="The Feature Phone Games" icon="arcticons:gree-plus"></iconheader>

There are two GREE feature phone games (_Danketsu_ and _Tensei_) that certainly exist somewhere. There are also efforts to create feature phone emulators that we know about.
If these games become playable at some point, we will likely translate them.

<iconheader title="The Promise of Haruhi Suzumiya (Yakusoku)" icon="simple-icons:playstationportable"></iconheader>

_Yakusoku_ (PSP, 2007) is the first Haruhi game to be released and also the only one to have a translation not made by us. We probably won't go back and re-translate it,
but at the very least we will likely make the existing patch for this game more easily accessible by hosting it on our website with appropriate credit.
